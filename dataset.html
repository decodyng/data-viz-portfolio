Project Dataset: Wikileak State Dep't Cables

For this project, I'll be working with the dataset of State Department cables released by Wikileaks in 2011. The data consists of 251,287 cables, which span in time from 1966 to 2010. Each cable consists of the following informative variables:


  • Date
  • Classification Status (Categorical: Secret/Classified/Unclassified)
  • Origin (Categorical: 272 Embassies)
  • Destination (Categorical: 272 Embassies; not always present)
  • Content (Textual data)

Links to the full dataset can be found on Wikileak's release page for the cables here. Though the data was obviously originally not meant for public consumption, it is not effectively in the public domain for the purpose of distributing visualizations, per Wikileak's advice to readers to "Pick out interesting events (in the cables) and tell others about them. Use twitter, reddit, mail whatever suits your audience best."
This dataset appealed to me for a few reasons. First, while I'd previously been aware in the abstract of the leaks made by Wikileaks in recent years, I'd lacked the tools to effectively dive into the leaks and extract the kind of information they were intended to provide. Now that I have those tools, I want to try to understand this data for myself. Specifically in the field of visualization, I believe this data has the potential to underwrite a wide variety of potential techniques, including network visualization, map visualization, as well as an underlying time series structure. In addition to potential, this dataset also provides the challenge of finding intelligent and effective ways to summarize textual data in order to visualize it.
